TerraTiles

Design Document

Intro

This is the design document for the game “TerraTiles.” It is a 2d, top down, fixed camera game.

Technology

  • Developed with Wolfie 2d game engine
  • Hosted on a Firebase website
  • Music made with BeepBox
  • Sound effects made with Audacity
  • Artwork made with Piskel

Backstory

You have been given a planet. The previous person messed it up and it is up to you to fix it.
Their assistant, who is now your assistant, will give you some advice, then you’ll be on your own. Good luck!

Objective

  • Initially, from space, the player tries to create new land.
  • Later, the player tries to surround a hazard (fire, disease, etc.) with countertiles (water, walls, etc.), in an effort to eliminate the hazard and preserve their world.
  • There will also be houses the player will have to protect. If they fully eliminate the hazards before it destroys the houses, they win the level.

Gameplay

  • This is a tiled environmental simulation. The world runs by itself and updates its state based on a timer.
  • At the center of the screen, there will be a hazard. The hazard grows, based on a timer.
  • The player tries to surround the hazard with something that will counter it, before it covers the whole screen.
  • Different tiles interact with each other differently.
    • Space – Hazard
    • Desert – Counter to Space, Hazard to Grass
    • Grass – Counter to Desert
    • Water – Counter to Fire
    • Fire – Counter to Disease+Mud, Hazard on top of Grass
    • Disease – Hazard on top of Mud
    • Mud – Hazard

Levels

Levels – hazard (the problem), counter (what the player places)

  • fertile soil, space v. desert
    • You start with fertile soil.
    • The previous terraformer left a crack in the land! Space is spreading.
    • Try surrounding it with desert.
  • fertile soil, desert v. grass
    • You saved your soil!
    • However, it’s drying out. Desert is spreading.
    • Place grass before it’s too late.
  • grass, fire v. water
    • You grew grass everywhere.
    • It’s on fire!
    • Put it out with water.
  • grass, mud v. desert
    • Flooding the grasslands has created a mud problem.
    • Surround it with desert?
  • mud, disease v. fire
    • It didn’t work. Your land is filled with mud.
    • All of that mud has led to disease.
    • Burn it out.
  • fertile soil, desert v. grass
    • All that fire turned your mud back into fertile soil.
    • Your fertile soil is drying out. Desert is spreading.
    • Place grass before it’s too late.

Controls

Mouse Click – Replace tile, interact with the menu options

ESC – pauses the game

Graphical User Interface

  • Splash Screen – depicts a world before terraforming, after terraforming, and in crisis
  • Main Menu
    • PLAY – start the game at Level 1
    • LEVELS – level select
    • CONTROLS – displays controls
    • HELP – displays the backstory, developers, cheat codes
  • In-Game Menu
    • RESUME – the current level will resume
    • LEVELS – back to the level select screen
    • CONTROLS – displays controls
    • HELP – displays the backstory, developers, cheat codes
    • QUIT – back to the splash screen
  • In-Game GUI
    • Hazard Timer – Top left corner
    • Player Timer – Top left corner

Artwork

The artwork will be original. We will be focused most on creating tiles.

  • Tiles
    • Space
    • Fertile Soil
    • Desert
    • Grass
    • Water
    • Fire
    • Disease
    • Mud
  • Other elements
    • Fertile Soil: background tile, not a hazard or counter
    • House – the world will be populated on top of some Grass tiles in Levels 2-6

Sound Effects

All sound effects will be original. Sounds will be made to coincide with the following:

  • Winning the level
  • Losing the level
  • Each tile (for hazards spreading or for placement)

Music

Different original music for roughly every two levels.
